Understanding Our Products

What Is THCa?

THCa (tetrahydrocannabinolic acid) is a naturally occurring cannabinoid synthesized in the trichomes of raw hemp flower. It is the acidic precursor to THC and represents the dominant cannabinoid form present in unprocessed cannabis and hemp plants.

Raw State

THCa

In its native state, THCa is non-intoxicating due to its molecular structure.

  • Non-psychoactive compound
  • Contains -COOH group
  • Limited CB1 receptor interaction
  • Naturally occurring in raw hemp
C22H30O4
Decarboxylation

Heat removes carboxyl group (-COOH)

Active State

THC

After decarboxylation, THC becomes bioavailable and receptor-active.

  • Psychoactive compound
  • No carboxyl group
  • Strong CB1 receptor binding
  • Activated through heat
